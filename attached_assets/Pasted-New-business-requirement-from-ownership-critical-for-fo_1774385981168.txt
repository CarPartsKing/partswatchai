New business requirement from ownership — critical 
for forecast accuracy.

Our company has locations that are "third call" stores. 
These locations only get orders for certain SKUs when 
other distributors are stocked out. Their sales history 
for those SKUs is artificially inflated — it represents 
residual market demand, not true organic demand for 
that location.

If we forecast based on this distorted history, we will 
overstock bad SKUs at underperforming locations and make 
poor purchasing decisions.

We need to add location performance classification 
to the system. Build this in two parts:

PART 1 — transform/location_classify.py

Classify each location on two dimensions:

1. Overall performance tier (1/2/3):
   - Tier 1: first-call locations — high fill rate, 
     high revenue, low returns, broad SKU coverage
   - Tier 2: second-call locations — solid performers, 
     moderate breadth
   - Tier 3: third-call locations — low fill rate, 
     narrow SKU coverage, high dependency on specific 
     categories that nobody else stocks

2. Per SKU + location demand quality score (0.0 to 1.0):
   - 1.0 = this location is consistently selling this 
     SKU in all market conditions — true organic demand
   - 0.5 = mixed signal — sometimes organic, sometimes 
     residual
   - 0.0 = this location only sells this SKU when 
     correlated stockouts exist at other locations 
     in the same market — pure residual demand

To detect residual demand:
   - When multiple locations in the same region 
     show simultaneous stockouts on a SKU, and a 
     third-call location shows a sales spike at the 
     same time — that spike is residual, not organic
   - Flag those sales events in sales_transactions 
     as is_residual_demand = TRUE
   - These flagged sales should be EXCLUDED from 
     forecast training data just like anomalies

Add is_residual_demand BOOLEAN DEFAULT FALSE 
to sales_transactions via migration 009.

Add to sku_master:
   - demand_quality_score per location (new table: 
     sku_location_demand_quality)
   - location_tier (1/2/3) to a new locations table

PART 2 — update forecast_lgbm.py and forecast_rolling.py

Add demand_quality_score as a feature in LightGBM.
Exclude is_residual_demand = TRUE records from 
all model training — same pattern as is_anomaly.

When generating forecasts for a Tier 3 location 
on a low demand_quality SKU, weight the forecast 
toward the regional average rather than that 
location's own history.

This makes the system understand not just what 
sells, but why it sells and whether that signal 
should be trusted.

New capability = new files. 
location_classify.py only classifies locations 
and demand quality. Nothing else changes except 
the exclusion filter in the forecasting models.
